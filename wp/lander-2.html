Knowing how to manage your finances is difficult for two reasons

  1. Rule Overload: There are so many government rules that dictate how much you can contribute. If you’re married, single, older, younger, have a specific income. Researching and staying on top of all these rules can sap up hours of your time. Meticulously tracking how much you contribute to make sure you don’t exceed the limits is also stressful and time-consuming.
  2. Lack of Practical Advice: Buy low-fee index funds, divide up your assets into different types of stocks and bonds, get a high yield savings account. If you’ve dug into financial planning at all, you’ve likely heard these terms and might even understand what they mean. But actually implementing them and taking action is where it gets tricky. Which financial institutions offer these high-yield savings accounts — which one is the best. Which specific funds should you buy. How do you even open an account. Where do you click. How do you navigate the various bank platforms.
